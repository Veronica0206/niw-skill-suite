# EB-2 Threshold Eligibility — Gate Check

*Referenced from SKILL.md Pattern 0. Read before any Dhanasar prong analysis.*
*Updated March 2026 per USCIS Policy Manual (Jan 2025 update, PA-2025-03).*

USCIS evaluates NIW merits only after confirming underlying EB-2 eligibility.
If this gate fails, the Dhanasar analysis is moot.

---

## Advanced Degree Route

**Flag as DEFICIENT if:**
- **Occupation-level analysis (Jan 2025 tightening):** The intended occupation
  is not clearly a "profession" requiring at least a bachelor's degree as a
  **standard entry requirement** (not just preferred). USCIS now explicitly
  reviews the occupation requirements, not just the petitioner's credentials.
  - FAILS: RN with PhD proposing nursing endeavor — standard RN positions
    do not require advanced degrees (O*NET confirms only managerial/advanced
    clinical nursing positions require advanced degrees)
  - FAILS: Engineer with PhD starting a bakery — "baker does not typically
    require bachelor's degree for entry" (USCIS Policy Manual example)
  - PASSES: Engineer with PhD proposing engineering R&D endeavor
- Post-degree experience is not documented as directly related to the specialty.
  **Per Jan 2025 update:** "Progressive experience in field unrelated to
  bachelor's degree does not equate to advanced degree in that field."
  - FAILS: Chemistry degree + 5 years restaurant management for chemistry endeavor
- Experience letters do not explicitly use the phrase **"full-time"** — USCIS
  now rejects letters documenting years of work without expressly stating
  full-time status. Partial or ambiguous language triggers automatic failure.
- Required progressive experience is not documented as occurring **after**
  the bachelor's degree was earned.
- **Experience-specialty mismatch (Jan 2025 tightening):** The 5 years of
  post-bachelor's experience must be in the **same specialty** as the proposed
  endeavor. Cross-field experience does not count.

> RFE language: "The petitioner must first establish statutory eligibility for
> EB-2... the update also explains that exceptional ability must relate to the
> same endeavor described in the NIW request."

## Exceptional Ability Route (6-criterion test)

**Flag as DEFICIENT if:**
- The six USCIS criteria for exceptional ability are not counted and mapped
  explicitly in the petition record.
- Exceptional ability is not tied to **the same endeavor** described in the
  NIW request — a mismatch between the EB-2 basis and the proposed endeavor
  is a standalone RFE trigger independent of the Dhanasar analysis.

**The six criteria (3 of 6 required):**
1. Official academic record showing degree related to the area of exceptional ability
2. Letters documenting 10+ years of full-time experience in the occupation
3. License to practice the profession (if applicable)
4. Commanding salary evidencing exceptional ability
5. Membership in professional associations requiring outstanding achievement
6. Recognition for achievements from peers, government entities, or professional/business organizations

---

## Exceptional Ability — Two-Step Evidentiary Analysis

**Per Kazarian v. USCIS (596 F.3d 1115) and USCIS Policy Manual:**
- Step 1: Does evidence meet regulatory criteria (3 of 6)?
- Step 2: Final merits determination — evaluate ALL evidence together for
  "expertise significantly above that ordinarily encountered"
- **Meeting 3 of 6 criteria alone is INSUFFICIENT** — quality and caliber matter
- If petition claims exceptional ability, verify BOTH steps are addressed

**Comparable evidence:** When regulatory criteria don't readily apply, petitioner
may submit "comparable evidence" — but must explain WHY it's comparable.
General assertions that criteria don't apply are unacceptable. Witness letters
alone are unpersuasive without corroboration.

## Common EB-2 Failure Modes

| Issue | Flag Level |
|---|---|
| Experience letter omits "full-time" phrase | HIGH — automatic failure risk |
| Exceptional ability claimed but EB-2 basis is advanced degree | Flag mismatch |
| Post-degree experience in a different specialty | MEDIUM → HIGH (tightened Jan 2025) |
| Bachelor's + experience route: experience pre-dates degree | HIGH — USCIS requires post-degree |
| Exceptional ability criteria not individually counted | MEDIUM — adjudicators count them explicitly |
| Occupation doesn't require bachelor's for standard entry | HIGH — occupation-level gate (Jan 2025) |
| Experience specialty doesn't match proposed endeavor | HIGH — same specialty required (Jan 2025) |
| 3 of 6 criteria met but quality not assessed (Step 2 missing) | MEDIUM — two-step analysis required |
